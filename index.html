Story Elements & Common Phrases
(故事元素与常用短语)

A bilingual guide for understanding narratives in Chinese and English media.

Introduction to Story Elements 📚

What Makes a Story?

Understanding the fundamental building blocks of storytelling is crucial for both creators and audiences. Whether you're analyzing a gripping drama, a thrilling novel, or a captivating film, recognizing these elements enhances your appreciation and comprehension.

了解讲故事的基本构成要素对于创作者和观众都至关重要。无论是分析引人入胜的戏剧、惊险的小说还是迷人的电影，识别这些元素都能增强您的欣赏和理解能力。

How to Use This Resource

This website serves as a learning tool, a quick reference for writers, and a guide for dissecting narratives in both Chinese and English shows and books. Each entry provides the English term, its Chinese translation with Pinyin, and explanations in both languages.

本网站可作为学习工具、作家的快速参考指南，以及剖析中英文影视作品和书籍叙事的指南。每个条目都提供英文术语、其对应的中文翻译和拼音，以及双语解释。

Core Story Elements 🧩

A. Plot Devices ⚙️

Plot Device (情节装置) (Qiéngjié Zhuāngzhì)

A specific element used to advance the plot, create conflict, or resolve issues within a story.

用于推动情节、制造冲突或解决故事问题的特定元素。

Trope (桥段) (Qiáoduàn)

A recurring theme or motif in storytelling that represents familiar concepts or patterns.

讲故事中经常出现的主题或动机，代表熟悉的概念或模式。

Cliché (老套) (Lǎotào)

Overused themes, phrases, or elements that have become predictable and lack originality due to their excessive repetition.

过度使用的主题、短语或元素，由于过度重复而变得可预测且缺乏原创性。

Chekhov's Gun (切霍夫的枪) (Qiēhuòfū de Qiāng)

A seemingly insignificant object or detail introduced early in the story that becomes crucially important later.

故事开头出现的一个看似微不足道的物品或细节，后来变得至关重要。

Deus Ex Machina (机器神) (Jīqì Shén)

An unexpected, improbable, or artificial solution that resolves a hopeless situation. Often seen as a weak plot device due to lack of internal logic.

一个意外、不可能或人为的解决方案，解决绝望的局面。经常被认为是弱点。

MacGuffin (马克戈芬) (Mǎkè Gēfēn)

Object or goal driving the plot and motivating characters, but its specific nature is less important than its pursuit.

驱动情节和激励角色的物品或目标，但其具体性质不如追求其本身重要。

Plot Coupon (情节代币) (Qíngjié Dàibiāo)

A more generic term for a MacGuffin, any item whose main purpose is to push the plot forward.

更通用的术语，指任何推动情节的物品。

Plot Armor (情节护甲) (Qíngjié Hùjiǎ)

Unrealistic protection for a main character, where they survive situations defying logic, reducing suspense and stakes.

对主角的不现实保护，使他们在违背逻辑的情况下生还，减少悬念和风险。

Red Herring (误导线索) (Wùdǎo Xiànsuǒ)

A false clue intentionally misleading the audience, creating surprise and shifting suspicion.

故意误导观众的虚假线索，产生惊讶并转移怀疑。

Ticking Clock (倒计时) (Dào Jìshí)

A time limit or rapidly approaching deadline raising tension and urgency in the plot.

时间限制或快速接近的最后期限增加情节的紧张感和紧迫感。

Death Trap (死亡陷阱) (Sǐwáng Xiànjǐng)

A seemingly inescapable situation with a countdown until the hero's demise, increasing suspense.

一个看似无法逃脱的局面，设有倒计时增加悬念。

B. Narrative Techniques & Structures 📖

Flashback (倒叙) (Dàoxù)

Interruption of the present narrative to depict past events, providing context or revealing backstory.

中断当前叙述以描绘过去事件，提供背景或揭示故事背景。

Flashforward (预示) (Yùshì)

Interruption of the current narrative to show a future event, hinting at possible outcomes or raising questions.

中断当前叙述以展示未来事件，暗示可能的结果或提出问题。

Foreshadowing (伏笔) (Fúbǐ)

Hints or clues about events to come, creating expectation and building suspense.

关于将来事件的暗示或线索，创造期望并增加悬念。

Frame Narrative (框架叙事) (Kuàngjià Xùshì)

A story within a story, providing an additional layer of perspective or context.

故事中的故事，提供额外的视角或背景。

In Medias Res (中途介入) (Zhōngtú Jiè Rù)

Starting the story in the middle of the action, hooking the reader and revealing the plot through exposition later.

从故事的中间开始，吸引读者并通过后续叙述揭示情节。

Narrative Hook (叙事钩子) (Xùshì Gōuzi)

A compelling element (event, question, character) introduced early to capture the audience's attention and make them want to keep reading.

早期引入的引人注目的元素（事件、问题、角色），吸引观众继续阅读。

Subplot (副情节) (Fù Qíngjié)

A secondary storyline running parallel to the main plot, often used to add complexity and explore different themes.

与主情节平行的次要情节线，通常用于增加复杂性并探索不同的主题。

Unreliable Narrator (不可靠的叙述者) (Bù Kěkào de Xùshù Zhě)

A narrator whose perspective is biased, flawed, or untrustworthy, making the reader question the truth they present.

一个偏见、缺陷或不可信的叙述者，使读者质疑他们呈现的真相。

Repetition (重复) (Chóngfù)

Repeating words, phrases, or images for emphasis and to create a rhythmic effect.

重复单词、短语或图像以强调和创造节奏感。

Montage (蒙太奇) (Méngtàijí)

A series of short scenes or images edited together to quickly show a passage of time or multiple events.

一系列短场景或图像被编辑在一起，快速展示时间的流逝或多个事件。

C. Tropes & Clichés 🤔

Cliffhanger (悬念) (Xuánniàn)

An ending with unresolved tension, creating suspense and a desire to continue the story.

以未解决的紧张情节结束，产生悬念并激发继续阅读的欲望。

Meet Cute (初遇) (Chū Yù)

Humorous or charming initial encounter between romantic leads, setting the stage for their relationship.

幽默或迷人的初次相遇，设定了他们关系的基础。

The Power of Love (爱的力量) (Ài de Lìliàng)

Love (familial, romantic, platonic) serves as a major motivation, overcoming obstacles or leading to redemption.

爱（家庭、浪漫、友谊）作为主要动机，克服障碍或导致救赎。

Poetic Justice (诗意正义) (Shīyì Zhèngyì)

A satisfying outcome where a character gets what they deserve, either a fitting reward or punishment.

一个令人满意的结果，角色得到应得的报应或奖励。

Quest (任务) (Rènwu)

A journey with a specific goal, often involving challenges and character growth.

一次有特定目标的旅程，通常涉及挑战和角色成长。

Self-Fulfilling Prophecy (自我应验的预言) (Zìwǒ Yìyàn de Yùyán)

A prediction or belief that directly or indirectly causes itself to become true.

一种预言或信念直接或间接导致其实现。

Prophecy (预言) (Yùyán)

A prediction about the future that shapes characters' actions and potentially sets events in motion.

关于未来的预测，塑造角色的行为并可能推动事件的发生。

Time Travel (时间旅行) (Shíjiān Lǚxíng)

Movement through time (past or future), altering the course of events, and exploring potential consequences.

穿越时间（过去或未来），改变事件的进程，并探讨潜在的后果。

Doppelganger (双胞胎) (Shuāng Bāotāi)

A mysterious double of a character, often with contrasting traits or representing a hidden aspect of the individual.

角色的神秘双胞胎，通常具有对比特质或代表隐藏的一面。

Mistaken Identity (误认身份) (Wù Rèn Shēnfèn)

A character is mistaken for someone else, leading to confusion, danger, intrigue, or comic situations.

角色被误认为另一个人，导致混乱、危险、谜团或喜剧情境。

D. Literary Devices (Figurative Language & Rhetorical Devices) ✒️

Metaphor (隐喻) (Yǐnyù)

An implied comparison between two things to emphasize a shared quality, without using "like" or "as".

对比两者以突出共同特质的隐含比较，不使用“像”或“如”。

Motif (主旨) (Zhǔzhǐ)

A recurring image, symbol, or idea that contributes to the overall theme of the work.

反复出现的图像、符号或理念，贡献于作品的整体主题。

Paradox (矛盾) (Máodùn)

A seemingly contradictory statement that reveals a deeper truth.

似乎矛盾的陈述揭示了更深层的真理。

Parallelism (对比) (Duìbǐ)

Using similar grammatical structures to highlight a connection between ideas or characters.

使用类似的语法结构突出思想或角色之间的联系。

Pathetic Fallacy (情感拟人化) (Qínggǎn Nǐrén Huà)

Attributing human emotions and responses to nature, often mirroring the mood of the story.

将人类情感和反应赋予自然，通常反映故事的情绪。

Situational Irony (情境讽刺) (Qíngjìng Fèngcì)

A contrast between what's expected and what actually happens, often with a humorous or darkly ironic twist.

期望和实际发生之间的对比，通常带有幽默或黑暗的讽刺。

Symbolism (象征主义) (Xiàngzhēng Zhǔyì)

Using an object, person, or event to represent a larger abstract idea.

使用物体、人物或事件代表更大的抽象概念。

Hyperbole (夸张) (Kuāzhāng)

Deliberate exaggeration for emphasis or humor, not meant to be taken literally.

故意的夸张以强调或幽默，不应字面理解。

Satire (讽刺) (Fèngcì)

Using humor, irony, or exaggeration to criticize society, individuals, or institutions.

使用幽默、讽刺或夸张来批评社会、个人或机构。

Reversal/Peripeteia (反转/突然转折) (Fǎnzhuǎn/Tūrán Zhuǎnzhe)

A sudden shift in a character's fortunes, from good to bad or vice versa.

角色运气的突然转变，从好到坏或反之亦然。

Twist (扭曲) (Niǔqū)

An unexpected turn of events in the conclusion that radically changes the audience's understanding of what preceded it.

结尾的意外转折，彻底改变观众对前情的理解。

Obstacle (障碍) (Zhàng'ài)

A challenge or problem the protagonist must overcome to progress towards their goal.

主人公必须克服的挑战或问题，以接近他们的目标。

Character Archetypes and Roles 🎭

Protagonist (主角) (Zhǔjué)

The main character around whom the story revolves.

故事围绕其展开的主要角色。

Antagonist (反派) (Fǎnpài)

The character or force that opposes the protagonist.

与主角对立的角色或力量。

Mentor (导师) (Dǎoshī)

A wise and experienced character who guides the protagonist.

指导主角的智慧且经验丰富的角色。

Sidekick/Loyal Companion (搭档/忠实伙伴) (Dādàng/Zhōngshí Huǒbàn)

A character who accompanies and supports the protagonist.

陪伴并支持主角的角色。

Foil (衬托人物) (Chèntuō Rénwù)

A character who contrasts with another character (usually the protagonist) to highlight particular qualities.

与另一个角色（通常是主角）形成对比，以突出特定品质的角色。

Love Interest (爱慕对象) (Àimù Duìxiàng)

A character with whom the protagonist develops a romantic relationship.

主角发展浪漫关系的角色。

Villain (恶棍) (Ègùn)

A character who commits evil acts or is responsible for much of the conflict.

作恶多端或对大部分冲突负责的角色。

Anti-Hero (反英雄) (Fǎn Yīngxióng)

A protagonist who lacks conventional heroic attributes but often acts heroically.

缺乏传统英雄特质但通常表现出英雄行为的主角。

Dynamic Character (动态角色) (Dòngtài Juésè)

A character who undergoes significant internal change throughout the story.

在故事中经历重大内部变化的角色。

Static Character (静态角色) (Jìngtài Juésè)

A character who remains largely unchanged throughout the story.

在故事中基本保持不变的角色。

Stock Character (定型角色) (Dìngxíng Juésè)

A stereotypical character audiences instantly recognize from other stories.

观众从其他故事中立刻认出的刻板角色。

Setting and Atmosphere 🏞️

Setting (背景) (Bèijǐng)

The time and place in which the story occurs.

故事发生的时间和地点。

Atmosphere/Mood (气氛/情绪) (Qìfēn/Qíngxù)

The emotional feeling or tone created by the setting and events.

由背景和事件营造的情感氛围或基调。

World-building (世界构建) (Shìjiè Gòujiàn)

The process of creating an imaginary world, often with its own rules, history, and cultures.

创造一个虚构世界的过程，通常有其自身的规则、历史和文化。

Themes and Messages 💡

Theme (主题) (Zhǔtí)

The central idea, message, or underlying meaning of a literary work.

文学作品的中心思想、信息或深层含义。

Moral (寓意) (Yùyì)

A lesson, especially one concerning right or wrong, that can be derived from a story.

从故事中得出的教训，特别是关于对错的教训。

Conflict (冲突) (Chōngtú)

The struggle between opposing forces that drives the plot.

推动情节发展的对立力量之间的斗争。

Man vs. Self (人与自我) (Rén yǔ Zìwǒ)

Internal struggle within a character.

角色内部的斗争。

Man vs. Man (人与人) (Rén yǔ Rén)

Conflict between two or more characters.

两个或多个角色之间的冲突。

Man vs. Society (人与社会) (Rén yǔ Shèhuì)

Conflict between a character and societal norms or institutions.

角色与社会规范或制度之间的冲突。

Man vs. Nature (人与自然) (Rén yǔ Zìrán)

Conflict between a character and natural forces.

角色与自然力量之间的冲突。

Man vs. Fate/Supernatural (人与命运/超自然) (Rén yǔ Mìngyùn/Chāozìrán)

Conflict between a character and destiny, gods, or supernatural entities.

角色与命运、神灵或超自然实体之间的冲突。

Narrative Arcs 🎢

Hero's Journey (英雄之旅) (Yīngxióng zhī Lǚ)

A common narrative pattern describing a hero's adventure, challenges, and return.

描述英雄冒险、挑战和回归的常见叙事模式。

Redemption Arc (救赎弧线) (Jiùshú Húxiàn)

A character's journey from wrongdoing or moral failure to atonement or positive change.

角色从错误或道德失败走向赎罪或积极变化的旅程。

Tragic Arc (悲剧弧线) (Bēijù Húxiàn)

A character's downfall due to a fatal flaw or external circumstances.

角色因致命缺陷或外部环境而衰落的弧线。

Tips for Analysis and Writing ✍️

How to Identify Plot Devices/Tropes

Look for patterns, recurring elements, and moments where the plot takes an unexpected turn or a character's journey follows a familiar path. Ask yourself: "Does this feel familiar?" or "How does this element move the story forward?"

寻找模式、重复元素，以及情节出现意想不到的转折或角色旅程遵循熟悉路径的时刻。问自己：“这感觉熟悉吗？”或“这个元素如何推动故事发展？”

Using Tropes Effectively

Tropes aren't inherently bad! They can be powerful shortcuts for conveying information. The key is to subvert them, add a fresh twist, or use them in a way that surprises the audience while still leveraging their familiarity.

桥段并非天生就坏！它们可以是传达信息的强大捷径。关键在于颠覆它们，加入新的转折，或者以一种在利用其熟悉性的同时又能让观众感到惊喜的方式使用它们。

Avoiding Clichés

While tropes can be used effectively, clichés often feel stale. To avoid them, focus on originality in character motivation, unique settings, and unexpected resolutions. Ask: "Has this been done countless times before?" If so, how can you make it truly yours?

虽然桥段可以有效利用，但老套的情节往往令人感到厌倦。为了避免它们，请专注于角色动机的原创性、独特的背景和意想不到的结局。问问自己：“这是否已经被无数次地使用过？”如果是，您如何才能使其真正属于您？
